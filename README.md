Marketing Campaign Dashboard

### Overview
This dashboard offers a comprehensive visualization and analysis of marketing campaign performance, customer segmentation, financials, and complaint trends. It is designed to assist marketing and analytics teams in evaluating campaign effectiveness, purchase channels, and identifying areas for improvement.

### Features
- *High-Level Metrics*: Displays total campaigns, income, spending, and unique IDs.
- *Campaign Distribution*: A pie chart summarizes the share of each campaign, helping track performance and audience reach.
- *Purchases Analysis*: Another pie chart highlights the breakdown of purchases by channel (Web, Catalog, Store, Deals).
- *Financial Trends*: Scatter and bar charts show income and spending trends by day and by quarter.
- *Customer Segmentation*: Education levels are analyzed to identify which segments respond best.
- *Complaint Tracking*: Bar charts illustrate complaint trends by quarter, helping monitor customer satisfaction over time.
- *Interactive Filters*: Education and Marital Status filters allow targeted analysis for deeper insights.

### How to Use
1. *Navigate Metrics*: Review headline numbers for overall campaign activity.
2. *Explore Distribution*: Use pie charts to understand which campaigns and purchase channels are leading.
3. *Analyze Financials*: Track income and spending by day or quarter to spot peaks, dips, and correlations.
4. *Examine Segments*: Leverage the education chart to tailor future campaigns to responsive demographics.
5. *Monitor Complaints*: Observe complaint trends to evaluate changes in strategy and address recurring issues.
6. *Apply Filters*: Use dropdown filters for Education and Marital Status to refine results and understand specific audience behavior.

### Interpretation and Insights
- Successful campaigns show strong performance in Crsp1 and Crsp2.
- Catalog and deals are the top-performing channels for purchases.
- Income is positively correlated with spending; both are increasing steadily.
- Most complaints occurred early (2013) and have since declined, showing improvements.
- Graduates and postgraduates are the highest-engaged customer segments.
